Research Overview

My doctoral research focuses on the modal and non-modal stability of pulsating Taylor–Couette flow using Floquet theory and direct–adjoint optimization techniques. My work investigates how time-periodic forcing modifies instability mechanisms, transient energy growth, and transition pathways in rotating shear flows.

My broader interests include hydrodynamic stability, spectral methods for partial differential equations, flow control, scientific computing, and high-performance numerical simulations.

Research Interests
